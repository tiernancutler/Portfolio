Lotus Rebranding and Website Resdesign

Overview

The Problem

I was given the job of rebranding Lotus Yoga Retreats’ company logo and their website.

My Role

UX/UI Designer and Graphic Designer

Starting Point

The Old Website and Branding

The Rebrand

Company Logo

The company wished to rename to simply just be Lotus. So, in turn, I wanted to simplify their logo to be cleaner and more modern. While sticking with an obvious choice of the lotus as the main symbol to be incorporated, and keeping that aquamarine color, it resulted in this.

Website Redesign

The Major Issues

  • The site was not mobile-friendly.
  • Breakpoints that were jarring to the visitor.
  • There also wasn’t a clear direction for the user who wish to book a trip which was the site’s main goal.
  • Dark header image
  • The images were not consistent in their overall feel and low-resolution.
  • Social images hard to find
  • Newsletter sign-up not prominent
  • Subtext color font did not pass WCAG requirements

The Solutions

  • Cut down on the first-level navigation to drive more users to the Retreats page. Moved less-important info into footer.
  • Have a clear CTA in the hero section to drive users to retreats landing page.
  • Include their accolades near the top of the page to instill trust in visitors early on.
  • Decluttered site with more white-space.
  • Focused on company’s selling points.
  • Included positive user review as, according to user surveys, 94% of consumers saying positive reviews make them more likely to use a business.
  • Added a mailing list form to grow contact list.
  • Used a 12-column grid to have more effective breakpoints for mobile viewing.
  • Updated color palette to meet all WCAG requirements
